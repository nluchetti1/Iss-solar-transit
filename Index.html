How this works & accuracy

A solar transit centerline is the ISS's own shadow track on the daylit ground: stand on it, look at the Sun through a certified solar filter, and the station crosses the disk. This tool propagates the TLE with SGP4, computes the Sun direction, and projects the shadow onto the WGS84 ellipsoid at your elevation. The time and distance at your nearest point are refined by golden-section search to well under a second.

Refraction is reported as the apparent Sun altitude but deliberately not applied to the centerline: the ISS orbits above the atmosphere, so the Sun and station refract together and the effect on the line largely cancels — forcing textbook Sun-refraction here would actually add kilometres of error at low Sun.

Remaining error is dominated by TLE age. With a <24 h element set the centerline is good to roughly a few hundred metres and the timing to a fraction of a second — close to transit-finder.com. Still, before a long drive, confirm the exact line with a dedicated finder using the same fresh TLE, and enter your true elevation above.
